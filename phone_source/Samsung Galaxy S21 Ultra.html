Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra Quick Review

Pros

  • Great Performance and 5G Support
  • Massive 5000 mAh Battery with Fast/Wireless Charging
  • On-Screen Fingerprint Sensor and Beautiful Design
Cons
  • No 3.5mm Headphone Jack

Overview

Samsung is finally done with unveiling its next-gen premium S series flagship with the Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra being the top tier and best yet device from the brand. The Galaxy S21 Ultra is evidently the most powerful and premium flagship from the lineup. This high-end device features some of the most powerful specs seen so far such as a stunning 120Hz AMOLED display, a fantastic camera setup, a massive 5000mAh battery, and a lot more. The device is indeed be powered by the latest Exynos 2100 chipset.

Design and Display

The Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra flaunts a best-in-class 6.8 inch Dynamic AMOLEDdisplay capped with a high resolution of 1440 x 3200 pixels, an incredibly high pixel density of 516ppi, and a screen to body ratio of 89.44% to fabricate stunning visuals. The device sports an immersive punch-hole design along with HDR10+ and a buttery smooth refresh rate of 120Hz to enhance the overall experience even further. The device also gets Corning Gorilla Glass vicyus protection and IP68 water resistance.

Cameras

The Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra boasts a powerful quad rear camera setup with a 108MP primary camera, a 10MP periscope camera, a 10MP telephoto sensor, and a 12MP ultra-wide lens with LED flash onboard. Upfront, the smartphone features a powerful 40MP selfie-shooter. The camera arrives with features such as AF Phase Detection, Auto Flash, Auto Focus, Body detection, Bokeh Effect, Continuos Shooting, Exposure compensation, Face detection, Geo tagging, High Dynamic Range mode (HDR), ISO control, ISOCELL Plus, Laser AF, Optical image stabilization, Touch to focus, White balance presets and modes like Panorama, Portrait Mode, and Pro Mode.

Battery and Performance

The Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra comes baked with Samsung's most powerful Exynos 2100 Octa-Core chipset. The device ships with a 12GB RAM paired with an internal storage capacity of 256GB, which is not expandable. Fuelling the device is a giant 5000mAh Li-Ion battery with Fast and Wireless Charging support onboard.

Software and Connectivity

Samsung Galaxy S21 Ultra runs Android 11 out of the box with One UI 3.1 on top. As far as connectivity is concerned, the smartphone supports a bunch of options like 5G, Dual sim VoLTE support (Nano + Nano), Wi-Fi 802.11, a,ac,b,g,n,n 5GHz, GPS, NFC, and Bluetooth 5.1. On top of that, the smartphone also comes with a USB Type-C port and an On-Screen fingerprint sensor as well.
